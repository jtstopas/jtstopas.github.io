plt.title("BA Close Price with 20 and 75-Day Moving Averages")
plt.legend()
plt.show()

Gc The 20-peroid (day) moving average is, of course, much closer to the actual daily closing price by design. They both have their pros and cons. Some pros of the 75-peroid (day) are that it smooths out the more dramatic variations and makes it easier to spot the overall trend. As a con it misses the precise mark during the best and worst times for the stock during the period it represents. The pros of the 20-peroid (day) moving average, as mentioned initially, is that it follows the actual data more closely. A con would be (well... sort of) that it doesnt smooth things out as nicely so it may be more difficult to visually establish a long-term trend in some cases. The longer periods are likely more useful for the investor who thinks in longer term cycles; the shorter periods perhaps more useful for those who prefer to make quick trades (i.e. shorter holding periods) and/or prefer options contracts with closer expiration dates.
